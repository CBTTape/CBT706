<axis>

Syntax

<axis>
    <x>
          .
          .
        <label>Jan</label>
        <label>Feb</label>
        <label>Mar</label>
        <label>Apr</label>
        <label>May</label>
        <label>Jun</label>
        <label>Jul</label>
        <label>Aug</label>
        <label>Sep</label>
        <label>Oct</label>
        <label>Nov</label>
        <label>Dec</label>
    </x>
    <y>
        <title>Y-axis Title</title>
        <range>
            <min>0</min>
        </range>
        <gridline>
            <major>true</major>
            <minor>true</minor>
        </gridline>
        <reference at="55" style="stroke:green;stroke-opacity:0.2;stroke-width:5;stroke-dasharray:none;">
            This is a horizontal reference line
        </reference>
        <reference at="36" style="stroke:red;stroke-dasharray:10,5,2,5;">
            This is another horizontal reference line
        </reference>
    </y>
    <x2>
      .
      .
    </x2>
    <y2>
      .
      .
    </y2>
</axis>

Synopsis

The <axis> tag describes the axes of the chart.

Under the <axis> tag you can insert:

  • an <x> tag describing the X-axis.
  • a <y> tag describing the Y-axis.
  • an <x2> tag describing the secondary X-axis.
  • a <y2> tag describing the secondary Y-axis.
Under each of the <x>, <y>, <x2> and <y2> tags you can insert:
  • a <title> tag specifying the axis title
  • a <range> tag specifying the axis range (minimum and maximum value plotted)
  • a <gridline> tag specifying the whether you want major and/or minor gridlines drawn on this axis.
  • any number of <reference> tags specifying that a reference line is to be drawn at a particular place on this axis.
  • a <label> tag to specify the text (rather than a numeric value) to appear at major gridline intervals on this axis.
